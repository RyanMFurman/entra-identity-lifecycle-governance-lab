# Portfolio and Interview Guide

## Two-minute explanation

Northstar needed reliable joiner/mover/leaver controls. I built a local digital twin in Python and SQLite with PowerShell entry points. The Jasmine mover scenario deliberately retains sensitive Finance access after an Engineering transfer. The validator finds stale access and a toxic combination, the investigation derives evidence from the audit trail, remediation recalculates desired access, and the same validator proves recovery.

## Resume bullets

- Built a deterministic IAM lifecycle digital twin with transactional state, auditable JML changes, SoD detection, remediation, and CI-tested recovery.
- Automated synthetic identity investigations and generated sanitized machine-readable and human-readable evidence reports.

## Portfolio copy

An executable identity-governance lab demonstrating how excessive access appears during a failed mover workflow and how policy-driven remediation restores compliance. Technologies: Python, PowerShell, SQLite, JSON, pytest, and GitHub Actions.

## Interview questions

- Why separate policy from adapters? To preserve one decision model across local and authorized execution.
- Why rerun the same validator? It proves remediation changed state rather than merely closing a ticket.
- What needs tenant validation? Licensing, Graph permissions, Conditional Access interactions, PIM, access reviews, SSO, and SCIM behavior.
